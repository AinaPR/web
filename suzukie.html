Suzuki Method

Method Principles

The Suzuki method is an instrumental teaching method created by Shinichi Suzuki. This makes a learning proposal parallel to learning the mother tongue where the child learns through interaction with others in a transmission of mainly oral knowledge. In this way the Suzuki method proposes to learn to play from listening, imitation and repetition. Based on this idea, it is possible that children of 2-3 years old can begin to learn the violin progressively incorporating the elements of musical language.

Precisely because of this social and community character typical of any language, the method combines individual classes with collective classes with other children. It also integrates parents in the classroom creating a triangle of learning interactions between the child, the parents and the teacher. In this way, a special bond is created where learning is generated through love and from a perspective that is always positive and constructive. The figure of parents is essential in the development of musical skills and in home study as a support and model for learning. In the same way, the education of children's hearing is very important with the daily listening of music.

The Suzuki method is especially sensitive to the individualities of the students, respecting their learning rhythm at the same time that their full potential is developed. We do not believe in talent as something innate, but rather as potential capacities that we have to cultivate every day. I encourage you to let yourself be carried away by this journey of learning not only about the instrument but also about values and sensitivity that will create a very special bond with music.
